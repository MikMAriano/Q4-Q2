They say it's more fun in the Philippines, so why don't we take a walk along it's most famous tourist destinations!

Puerto Galera

Puerto Galera is primarily famous for its splendid beaches, coral reefs teeming with marine life and exquisite dive sites. The region was declared a Marine Reserve by the United Nations in 1974, owing to its abundance of exotic fish species seldom seen elsewhere.

Bohol

The home of the famous Chocolate Hills, Bohol is one of the most visited destinations in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. The island province offers breathtaking spots for history buffs, beach lovers, and adrenaline junkies.



Batanes

It is known for its scenic waters and terrain and clean environment. Batanes is also recognized for the unique culture of the islands' first settlers, the Ivatans. Batanes is open for swimming, snorkeling, diving, lighthouse visits, biking, and visits to century-old Ivatan houses.
